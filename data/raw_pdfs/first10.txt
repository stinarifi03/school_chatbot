EPOKA UNIVERSITY DOCUMENTS SUMMARY

CORE DOCUMENTS

1. BASIC REGULATION (EU-REG-001-EN)
Purpose: Foundation document governing all university operations
Key Points:
- Structure: 3 Faculties, Departments, Research Centers, Administrative Units
- Governing Bodies: Administrative Board, Academic Senate, Rectorate, Ethics Board
- Management: Rector (highest academic authority), Deans, University Administrator
- Language: English is official communication language
- Academic System: American system with Bologna ECTS compliance
- Academic Year: Fall & Spring semesters (16 weeks each)
- Instruction: English language (exceptions possible)
- Student Status: Must sign Student Individual Contract, not enrolled elsewhere
- Admission Criteria: Based on State Matura, CGPA requirements, English proficiency
- Grading: AA-FF scale with GPA calculation (CGPA ≥ 2.00 for graduation)
- Academic Staff: Professors, Lecturers, Assistant Lecturers with defined recruitment criteria

2. CODE OF ETHICS (EU-COD-001-EN)
Purpose: Defines ethical standards for all university community members
Core Values:
- Universality of education and research
- Respect for diverse religions, languages, genders, nationalities
- No compromise on justice and integrity
- Student-focused institution
- Pursuit of excellence
- Original research emphasis
Key Principles:
- Academic freedom with responsibility
- Honesty in research and authorship
- Responsible use of university resources
- Ethical conduct outside university
- Integrity in teaching and research
- Tolerance and openness to new ideas

ACADEMIC OPERATIONS

3. EXAMINATION GUIDELINE (EU-GDL-003-EN)
Purpose: Regulates all examination processes
Key Roles:
- University: Provides infrastructure, policies, exam environments
- Faculty: Announces schedules (2 weeks advance), manages conflicts, provides invigilators
- Department: Prepares exam materials, oversees conduct
- Course Instructor: Prepares exams, supervises, evaluates, ensures security
- Invigilator: Monitors exams, prevents cheating, maintains integrity
- Student: Must arrive on time, respect rules, no cheating, show ID
Exam Types: Written, Oral, Take-home, Jury, Open Book, Multiple Choice
Quality Assurance: Random exam assessment by assigned experts
Cheating: Defined as fraud, plagiarism, impersonation - leads to disciplinary action

4. COVID-19 ADAPTED EXAM GUIDELINE (EU-GDL-011-EN)
Purpose: Special provisions for 2020-2021 Fall semester remote exams
Exam Types:
1. Remote written test with pen/paper (via Microsoft Teams/Google Meet)
2. Remote computer-based test
3. Open book/open source exam (via Google Classroom/email)
4. Project/Essay/Assignment submission
Requirements: Camera/microphone must be active, sessions recorded
Duration: Max 90 minutes (except projects)
Documentation: Evidence must be submitted to Department Coordinator

5. PROFESSIONAL PRACTICE GUIDELINE (EU-GDL-007-EN)
Purpose: Regulates professional practice for Bachelor and Integrated Master students
Key Roles:
- Department Supervisor: Academic staff member who guides student
- Site Supervisor: Professional at practice institution
- Career Planning Office: Provides placement information
- Student: Must secure placement, maintain notebook, submit final report
Process:
1. Student finds placement with supervisor assistance
2. Practice Plan developed with goals/objectives
3. Weekly notebook maintained and signed by site supervisor
4. Final report submitted and evaluated
5. Presentation to department committee
Documents Required: Approval Form, Notebook, Performance Assessment Form

6. GRADUATION WORK DIRECTIVE (EU-DIR-012-EN)
Purpose: Regulates diploma thesis for undergraduate programs
Objectives:
- Develop analytical thinking and practical application
- Develop independent research methodology
- Demonstrate preparation for professional work
Process:
- Topics proposed by department and approved by Faculty
- Advisor assigned by Head of Department
- Can be individual or group work
- Defense in last two weeks of academic calendar
- Commission of ≥3 members evaluates
Requirements: Follows Directive on Diploma Thesis standards

ADMINISTRATIVE & SPECIAL UNITS

7. CONTINUOUS EDUCATION CENTER (EU-DIR-008-EN)
Purpose: Provides lifelong learning and professional development
Activities:
- Seminars and courses for individuals/institutions
- Counseling services
- Project implementation
- Graduate degree programs
- Accredited programs for professionals
Structure:
- Director (appointed by Rector)
- Deputy Director
- Advisory Board (members from each faculty)
- Center Specialist
Financial Model:
- Income distribution: 38% University, 60% staff, 2% motivation reward
- Special rates for free seminars, commercial clients, partner institutions
- Motivation rewards: 50% Director, 35% Deputy, 15% Specialist

8. PERFORMANCE ASSESSMENT DIRECTIVE (EU-DIR-021-EN)
Purpose: Assesses academic and administrative performance for quality improvement
Boards:
- ACAQIB: Academic Performance Assessment Board
  - Members: One from each faculty, research centers, PCQA member, external expert
  - Evaluates academic performance every 2 years
- ADAQIB: Administrative Performance Assessment Board
  - Members: 3-5 members including HR Head and PCQA member
  - Evaluates administrative performance annually
Remuneration: Based on teaching load, research publications, administrative work
Awards: Highest-ranking staff in each category receive awards
External Evaluation: University can apply for ISO Quality Certification

9. RESEARCH PROJECTS GUIDELINE (EU-GDL-001-EN)
Purpose: Guidelines for internal and external research projects
Internal Projects Eligibility:
- Full-time academic staff with Master's degree or higher
- Research center members
- PhD students
Budget Categories:
- Administration (up to 10%)
- Equipment/Materials (up to 70%)
- Overheads (up to 10%)
- Mobility (up to 50%)
- Dissemination (up to 20%)
- Publication (up to 50%)
Evaluation: Threshold score 70/100 for funding
External Projects: Staff encouraged to participate, must align with university strategy
Ethics: No scientific fraud, plagiarism, or data fabrication tolerated

COVID-19 RESPONSE

10. COVID-19 TEACHING GUIDELINE (EU-GDL-014-EN)
Purpose: 2021-2022 Fall semester teaching during pandemic
Key Measures:
- Vaccination Requirement: Staff/students must show vaccination certificate or negative PCR test
- Access Control: Temperature checks, masks required indoors
- Teaching Mode: On-campus classes with safety measures
- Attendance: 60% minimum for Bachelor, 60% Master, 80% PhD
- Exams: All exams on-campus with safety protocols
- Meetings: Can be in-person with distancing or remote
- Library/Services: With safety measures, appointment systems encouraged
- Transportation: Encouraged private vehicles, bus service with registration
Enforcement: Verbal/written warnings, disciplinary proceedings for non-compliance

KEY POLICIES & PROCEDURES

Academic Policies:
- Registration Renewal: Required each semester, tied to tuition payment
- Course Load: Max 45 ECTS per semester
- Pre-requisites: Defined by departments
- Course Drop/Change: Within first 2 weeks of semester
- Grade Appeals: Within 5 days of grade announcement
- Leave of Absence: Max 4 semesters, with conditions
- Transfers: Possible after 1 semester, within university or from external
- Scholarships: Governed by separate directive

Staff Policies:
- Recruitment: Based on point system for publications, presentations, experience
- Promotion: Criteria defined in separate regulations
- Teaching Load: Regulated by specific directive
- Research Support: Through internal and external projects
- Performance Evaluation: Regular assessment for quality improvement

Student Support:
- Academic Advising: Each student assigned an advisor
- Disability Services: Accompanying service available
- Exchange Programs: Opportunities with partner universities
- Career Services: Through Career Planning and Alumni Office
- Graduate Support: Alumni relations through CEC

COMPLIANCE & ENFORCEMENT

All documents emphasize:
- Compliance with Albanian Law 80/2015 on Higher Education
- Adherence to University Statute
- Implementation ensured by Rector
- Regular updates and amendments
- Publication on university website
- Training and awareness for all stakeholders
