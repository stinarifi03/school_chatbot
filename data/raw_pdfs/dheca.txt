## 1. Directive on Student Clubs (EU-DIR-005-EN)\
**Objective**: To regulate the establishment, organization, and functioning of student clubs, ensuring student participation in extracurricular scientific, social, cultural, and sports activities.\
\
### Establishment and Registration (Art. 3)\
* **Minimum Requirement**: At least six students must submit a request.\
* **Documentation**: Requires a draft-statute and a signed document from a staff member agreeing to be the **Student Club Advisor**.\
* **Review**: The activity field cannot overlap with existing clubs. Approval is granted by the **Dean of Students**.\
\
### Membership and Participation (Art. 5)\
* Open to all matriculated students. Students can join multiple clubs.\
* Alumni can be "Honor Members" (no voting rights).\
* "Active Members" are those participating for at least two semesters.\
\
### Monitoring and Closure (Art. 10-11)\
* **Program Submission**: Clubs must submit a draft-program and budget at the start of each year.\
* **Inactivity Warning**: Clubs inactive for two months after the start of the year receive a written warning.\
* **Closure**: The Dean of Students can close a club for failure to comply with warnings or statutes.\

## 2. Directive on Student Transfer (EU-DIR-017-EN)\
**Objective**: Procedures for Intra-University (internal) and Inter-University (from other HEIs) transfers.\
\
### Application and Decisions (Art. 3-4)\
* **Timeline**: Applications follow the academic calendar.\
* **Response**: Decisions are typically made within **one week** of document submission to the Admission Office.\
* **Approval**: Reviewed by the Department and approved by the Faculty Decanate.\
\
### Bachelor and Master Transfer Specifics (Art. 5)\
* **Bachelor**: Transfers possible between 2nd-6th semesters (3-year) or 8th semester (4-year). Minimum course grade for credit: **1.00/4.00**.\
* **Master**: Transfers only at the start of the year. Requires a minimum **CGPA of 2.00/4.00**. Minimum course grade for credit: **2.50/4.00**.\
\
### Grade Conversion (Art. 8)\
* **Intra-transfer**: Grades are recognized as letter grades.\
* **Inter-transfer**: Grades are recognized as "T" (Transfer) grades. CGPA for graduation is based only on courses taken at Epoka.\
\

## 3. Directive on Staff Assignment to Duty (EU-DIR-007-EN)\
**Objective**: Regulates domestic and international task assignments for academic staff.\
\
### Assignment Categories (Art. 2)\
* **Temporary Duty**: Up to **1 month** (scientific meetings/research). Over 15 days requires Faculty Board permission and a detailed report.\
* **Long-term Duty**: Over **1 month** (research or graduate studies). Requires approval from both Faculty and University Administrative Boards.\
\
### Reporting and Obligations (Art. 6-7)\
* **Seniority**: Requires 3 years at Epoka and 1 year of teaching for long-term assignments.\
* **Reports**: Quarterly reports for graduate studies. A final report must be presented one month before the assignment ends.\
* **Repayment**: If a staff member leaves before finishing compulsory service, they must repay the scholarship/remuneration received.\
\

## 4. Directive on the Exam Board (EU-DIR-020-EN)\
**Objective**: Ensuring the quality of examinations through monitoring and evaluation.\
\
### Key Definitions (Art. 3)\
* **Additional Exam**: For students at the graduation stage with FD/FF in max two courses and CGPA < 2.00.\
* **Resit Exam**: Organized after final exams for students needing to improve failing grades.\
* **Final Exam**: Written/completed assessment after the last day of classes.\
\
### Responsibilities (Art. 8-9)\
* **Meetings**: Held regularly to evaluate grade distribution, exam forms, and the quality of tests.\
* **Fraud**: Monitoring and reporting fraud cases (cheating, plagiarism).\
* **Reporting**: An annual report is submitted to the Academic Senate covering exam quality and improvement proposals.\
\

## 5. Directive on the Library (EU-DIR-002-EN)\
**Objective**: Protecting and managing library resources for students and staff.\
\
### Rules of Conduct (Art. 4)\
* **Restrictions**: No food, beverages, or tobacco. Mobile phones must be silenced.\
* **Guest Use**: Outside users need permission from the Library Administration.\
* **Damage**: Users are financially liable for damaged or lost resources.\
\
### Lending and Penalties (Art. 5-10)\
* **ID**: University ID is mandatory for borrowing.\
* **Recalls**: Borrowed items can be recalled at any time and must be returned within **3 days**.\
* **Sanctions**: Violations lead to a **one-year library ban**, official reports, and disciplinary investigations.\
\

## 6. Promotion of Scientific Publications (EU-DIR-004-EN)\
**Objective**: Encouraging academic staff to publish under the Epoka University name.\
\
### Eligibility and Points (Art. 2-5)\
* **Affiliation**: Publications must explicitly name Epoka University.\
* **Commission**: The Publication Promotion and Scientific Activities Support Commission manages rewards.\
* **Standard Reward**: Journals in Q1, Q2, and Q3 receive points based on Impact Factor ($600 \\times \\text\{Factor\}$ for Q1). Minimum reward is 600 points.\
* **Limit**: Total annual rewards cannot exceed one month's gross salary.\
\
### Mandatory Acknowledgment (Art. 7)\
* All supported works must include: *"This article/paper/book/chapter was partially/fully supported by research funds from EPOKA University."*\

## 7. Directive on the Teaching Load (EU-DIR-011-EN)\
**Objective**: Defining weekly teaching hours for full-time and adjunct staff.\
\
### Weekly Load Calculation (Art. 2)\
If not defined by contract:\
* **Rector**: 6 hours.\
* **Vice-Rector / Dean**: 9 hours.\
* **Head of Dept / Director**: 12 hours.\
* **Professors / Lecturers (PhD)**: 16 hours.\
* **Assistant Lecturers**: 18 hours.\
\
### Payment Forms (Art. 3)\
* **Full-time**: Submit **Lecturer Fee Request Form** monthly to the Department Coordinator.\
* **Adjunct**: Department Coordinator fills the **Teaching Load Form** monthly.\
* **Approval**: Requires **University Administrator** approval before processing by Finance.\
\

## 8. Directive on Yunus Center (YCSBS) (EU-DIR-019-EN)\
**Objective**: Governing the mission and bodies of the Yunus Center for Social Business and Sustainability.\
\
### Mission and Governance (Art. 5-12)\
* **Mission**: Building awareness of social business to address social and environmental issues.\
* **Director**: Appointed by the Rector for a **3-year term** (renewable).\
* **Administrative Board (AB)**: Represent at least three base units. Duties include implementing the strategic plan and preparing annual financial reports.\
\
### Finance (Art. 13)\
* The Rector oversees financial management.\
* Income is distributed between the Center and University based on **Working Capital principles**.\

## 9. Data Protection and Privacy Policies (PLC-)\
**Objective**: Ensuring lawful processing of personal data in compliance with Law No. 124/2024 and GDPR.\
\
### Core Principles (Data Protection Art. 4 / Privacy Art. 2)\
* **Transparency**: Data processed lawfully and fairly.\
* **Purpose & Minimization**: Collected only for specific reasons; only necessary data is kept.\
* **Storage**: Data is deleted once the purpose is fulfilled.\
\
### Roles and Rights (Art. 3 & 11)\
* **DPO**: Oversees compliance and handles subject requests.\
* **Rights**: Right to access, rectify (correct), erase (delete), and data portability.\
* **Transfers**: International transfers require standard contractual clauses or proof of adequate protection in the recipient country.\
\

## 10. Exam Guidelines (Mid-term, Final, Resit - Pandemic Modalities)\
**Objective**: Procedures for online and remote exams (EU-GDL-005, 008, 011, 013).\
\
### Management of Evidence\
* **Submission Deadline**: Evidence must be submitted to the Department Coordinator within **5 working days** of grade entry in EIS.\
* **Documentation Package**:\
    * Answer keys and signed Grade Lists.\
    * Signed Attendance List (must note the "Modality" e.g., Online).\
    * **Digital Proofs**: A CD with exam files, email logs, and online platform screenshots.\
\
### Equality (Mid-term Art. 2.1)\
* Modalities ensure fairness despite the lack of full invigilation and prevent negative impacts from technical issues (internet/power cuts).\

## 11. Professional Practice for Professional Master (EU-GDL-004-EN)\
**Objective**: Regulates the Professional Practice course for PM programs.\
\
### Key Roles\
* **Department Supervisor**: Epoka staff who evaluates the final report.\
* **Site Supervisor**: External mentor at the organization/company.\
\
### Evaluation Criteria\
* Evaluated on: **Interest in Job**, **Attendance**, and **Performance**.\
* **Grades**: (A) Excellent, (B) Good, (C) Fair, (D) Poor, (F) Unsatisfactory.\

## 12. CCTV System Guidelines\
**Objective**: Management of campus surveillance for safety and security.\
\
### Technical Details\
* **Infrastructure**: IP network using **Netgear Prosafe FS728TP switches** (24 ports, PoE).\
* **Software**: **Surveillance Station Client** manages up to 40 cameras.\
\
### Data and Access\
* **Control Room**: Restricted access; staff must be verified.\
* **Disclosure**: Images only shared with third parties (e.g., Police) for legal/crime investigation reasons.\
* **Rights**: Subjects can request access to their own recorded images.}
