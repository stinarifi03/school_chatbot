## 1. Directive on Student Clubs (EU-DIR-005-EN)\
**Objective**: Regulates the establishment, organization, and functioning of student clubs to ensure active participation in extracurricular activities (scientific, social, cultural, and sports).\
\
### Establishment and Registration (Article 3)\
* **Application**: At least **six students** must submit a request to the Student Clubs and Activities Office.\
* **Documents Required**: A draft-statute of the club and a signed document from a staff member agreeing to act as the **Student Club Advisor**.\
* **Restrictions**: The activity field cannot overlap with or be very similar to an existing club.\
* **Approval**: Evaluated and approved by the **Dean of Students**.\
\
### Membership and Participation (Article 5)\
* **Eligibility**: Open to all matriculated students. A student may be a member of more than one club.\
* **Honor Members**: Alumni may participate as honor members but do not have voting rights.\
* **Active Status**: To be considered an "Active Member," a student must participate in club activities for at least two semesters.\
\
### Operations and Closure (Article 10)\
* **Program and Budget**: Clubs must submit a draft-program and budget (planned costs and funding sources) at the start of each academic year.\
* **Inactivity**: Clubs that do not undertake activities or hold meetings within **two months** of the start of the academic year receive a written warning.\
* **Closure**: If a club fails to comply after a warning, the Dean of Students may decide to close the club.\

## 2. Directive on Student Transfer (EU-DIR-017-EN)\
**Objective**: Outlines procedures for transfers within Epoka (Intra-University) and from other institutions (Inter-University).\
\
### General Terms (Article 3 & 4)\
* **Timeline**: Applications must be submitted during the period specified in the academic calendar.\
* **Decisions**: Typically rendered within **one week** of document submission to the Admission Office.\
* **Process**: Applications are reviewed by the respective Department and approved by the Faculty Decanate.\
\
### Bachelor Transfer Requirements (Article 5.1.1)\
* **Timing**: Transfers are possible between the 2nd and 6th semesters (3-year programs) or 8th semester (4-year programs).\
* **Grade Criteria**: Credits are recognized only for courses with a grade of at least **1.00/4.00**.\
\
### Master Transfer Requirements (Article 5.1.2)\
* **Timing**: Only at the beginning of each academic year.\
* **GPA**: Requires a minimum **CGPA of 2.00/4.00** (equivalent to 70/100).\
* **Grade Criteria**: Courses must have a grade of at least **2.50/4.00** for credit recognition.\
\

## 3. Directive on Staff Assignment to Duty (EU-DIR-007-EN)\
**Objective**: Regulates domestic and international task assignments for academic staff.\
\
### Assignment Types (Article 2)\
* **Temporary Duty**: Up to **1 month** for scientific meetings or research. Assignments over 15 days require Faculty Board permission and a detailed report.\
* **Long-term Duty**: Over **1 month** for research, applications, or graduate studies. Requires approval from both Faculty and University Administrative Boards.\
\
### Requirements and Reporting (Article 4 & 7)\
* **Seniority**: Long-term assignments require **3 years of seniority** at Epoka and at least 1 year of teaching experience.\
* **Deadlines**: Applications for board-approved duties must be submitted **20 days** in advance; others **10 days**.\
* **Reports**: Quarterly reports are required for graduate studies. A final report must be submitted **one month before** the assignment ends.\
\

## 4. Directive on the Exam Board (EU-DIR-020-EN)\
**Objective**: Ensures examination quality through monitoring and evaluation.\
\
### Key Definitions (Article 3)\
* **Additional Exam**: For graduating students with FD or FF in max two courses and a CGPA < 2.00.\
* **Resit Exam**: Organized immediately after finals for students with low grades.\
* **Fraud**: Includes plagiarism, falsification of data, cheating, and deceit.\
\
### Board Responsibilities (Article 4 & 8)\
* **Composition**: Includes a Chairperson and representatives from each Faculty.\
* **Meetings**: The board meets regularly to monitor exam schedules, evaluate grade distributions, and review fraud cases.\
* **Appeals**: Evaluates formal grade appeals submitted by students.\

## 5. Directive on the Library (EU-DIR-002-EN)\
**Objective**: Efficient management and protection of library resources.\
\
### Use and Conduct (Article 4)\
* **Forbidden**: Food, beverages, tobacco, and noise (including mobile phones).\
* **Guests**: May use resources only with permission from Library Administration.\
* **Liability**: The user is responsible for any damage or loss of materials.\
\
### Borrowing Policies (Article 5 & 10)\
* **ID Requirement**: Users must present a University ID to borrow.\
* **Non-borrowable**: Theses, dictionaries, encyclopedias, and unbound journals (may only be photocopied).\
* **Recalls**: Borrowed items can be recalled at any time; the user must return them within **3 days**.\
* **Sanctions**: Violating rules or damaging materials can result in a **one-year library ban** and disciplinary action.\
\

## 6. Promotion of Scientific Publications (EU-DIR-004-EN)\
**Objective**: Encourages staff to publish and engage in scientific activities.\
\
### Eligibility (Article 2 & 4)\
* **Affiliation**: Works must be published under the **Epoka University name**.\
* **Timeline**: Applications apply to the current or previous academic year.\
* **Acknowledgments (Art. 7)**: Must include: *"This article/paper/book/chapter was partially/fully supported by research funds from EPOKA University"*.\
\
### Reward Point System (Article 5)\
* **Q1 Journals**: $600 \\times \\text\{Impact Factor\}$.\
* **Q2 Journals**: $600 \\times (\\text\{Impact Factor\} \\div 2)$.\
* **Q3 Journals**: $600 \\times (\\text\{Impact Factor\} \\div 3)$.\
* **Minimum**: Eligible journals receive at least **600 points**.\
* **Cap**: Total annual rewards cannot exceed **one month\'92s gross salary** per staff member.\
\

## 7. Directive on the Teaching Load (EU-DIR-011-EN)\
**Objective**: Criteria for calculating weekly teaching hours for all study cycles.\
\
### Standard Weekly Loads (Article 2)\
* **Rector**: 6 hours.\
* **Vice-Rector / Dean**: 9 hours.\
* **Head of Department / Base Unit / Director**: 12 hours.\
* **Professor Category (Prof. / Assoc. Prof.)**: 16 hours.\
* **Lecturer (PhD holders)**: 16 hours.\
* **Assistant Lecturer**: 18 hours.\
\
### Fees and Payments (Article 3)\
* **Full-time Staff**: Submit the **Lecturer Fee Request Form** monthly to the Department Coordinator.\
* **Adjunct Staff**: The Department Coordinator fills out the load form for them monthly.\
* **Approval**: Forms must be approved by the **University Administrator** before Finance processes them.\
\

## 8. Directive on Yunus Center (YCSBS) (EU-DIR-019-EN)\
**Objective**: Governance of the Yunus Center for Social Business and Sustainability.\
\
### Mission and Governing Bodies\
* **Mission (Art. 5)**: Building social business awareness to solve economic and environmental issues.\
* **Director (Art. 7)**: Appointed by the Rector for a **3-year term** (renewable).\
* **Administrative Board (AB) (Art. 11-12)**:\
    * Must represent at least **three different base units**.\
    * Responsibilities: Implement the strategic plan, prepare annual reports, and coordinate external audits.\
\
### Finance (Article 13)\
* Financial management is under the Rector's responsibility.\
* Income from projects/publications is shared between the Center and University based on **Working Capital principles**.\
\

## 9. Data Protection Policy (04.06.2025)\
**Objective**: Secure and lawful processing of personal data (GDPR-aligned).\
\
### Core Principles (Article 4)\
* **Lawfulness**: Data is processed fairly and transparently.\
* **Purpose Limitation**: Collected only for explicit and legitimate purposes.\
* **Data Minimization**: Only necessary data is collected.\
* **Storage**: Kept only as long as needed for the purpose.\
\
### Roles and Rights (Article 3 & 11)\
* **DPO**: Ensures compliance and best practices.\
* **Data Subjects**: Have rights to **access, correct, delete, and port** their data.\
* **Staff**: Must attend regular training and report any protection concerns to the DPO.\
\

## 10. Privacy Policy (04.06.2025)\
**Objective**: Transparency on data collection and security.\
\
### Management (Article 3, 6 & 8)\
* **Data Types**: Names, birth dates, national ID numbers.\
* **Security**: Uses encryption and restricts access to authorized personnel only.\
* **Cookies**: Used for site performance; users can manage settings in their browser.\
* **International Transfer**: Allowed only with proper safeguards (e.g., standard protective clauses).\

## 11. Guidelines for Exam Conduct (EU-GDL-008 & 011)\
**Objective**: Modalities for Final/Resit exams (originally adapted for pandemic health protection).\
\
### Documentation Management\
* **Deadline**: Evidence must be submitted within **5 working days** of entering grades into the EIS system.\
* **Required Evidence**:\
    * Answer keys and signed Grade Lists.\
    * Signed Attendance Lists (noting the exam modality).\
    * **Digital Evidence**: A CD with exam materials, email logs, or platform screenshots.\
* **Collection**: The Department Coordinator ensures all items are in the exam envelope.}
