SCHOLARSHIPS POLICY
====================

DIRECTIVE "ON SCHOLARSHIPS" (EU-DIR-001-EN)
Last Updated: 05.04.2023

Purpose: Defines scholarship types and criteria for economic support to students.

Definition: Scholarships are economic support instruments offering full or partial tuition fee discounts.

SCHOLARSHIP TYPES FOR BACHELOR/INTEGRATED MASTER STUDENTS:
--------------------------------------------------------------
1. Merit-Based Scholarships:
   - Based on high school GPA, Matura State Exam, or SAT results
   - Discounts: 100%, 75%, or 50%

2. Special Category Scholarships:
   - Students with Disabilities
   - Children of Albanian policemen killed in duty (State Police list)
   - Orphans from low-income families
   - Roma and Egyptian community members
   - Sportsmen with high Olympic sports results
   - High-achieving graduates from Vora Municipality high schools

3. Continuity Requirements:
   For scholarships < 75%:
   - Maintain CGPA ≥ 2.50/4.00
   - If CGPA drops below 2.50: scholarship halved next year
   - If CGPA returns to ≥ 2.50: regain full scholarship
   - If remains below 2.50: lose scholarship completely

   For scholarships ≥ 75%:
   - Maintain CGPA ≥ 2.75/4.00
   - Similar reduction/recovery rules apply

4. Other Requirements:
   - Complete studies in normal duration
   - No study interruption
   - Active participation in extracurricular activities
   - Adherence to Code of Ethics and discipline
   - No horizontal transfers within university
   - Represent university with integrity

SCHOLARSHIPS FOR MASTER'S STUDENTS:
-------------------------------------
Based on Bachelor CGPA:

EPOKA University Graduates:
- CGPA 2.70-2.99: 10% discount
- CGPA 3.00-3.29: 20% discount
- CGPA 3.30-3.49: 30% discount
- CGPA 3.50-3.99: 40% discount
- CGPA 4.00 or top-ranked: 50% discount

External Graduates (Albanian system):
- CGPA 8.50-8.99: 10% discount
- CGPA 9.00-9.49: 20% discount
- CGPA 9.50-9.99: 30% discount
- CGPA 10.00: 40% discount

Continuity: Discount applies for normal study duration only.

ADMINISTRATION:
- Scholarship Commission established annually by Rectorate
- Criteria and quotas determined by Administrative Board
- Enforcement by University Administrator

SPECIAL STUDENTS POLICY
========================

DIRECTIVE "ON SPECIAL STUDENTS" (EU-DIR-018-EN)
Last Updated: 29.04.2013

Purpose: Regulates non-degree seeking students taking specific courses.

DEFINITION: Students not enrolled in degree programs but allowed to take courses.

ELIGIBILITY REASONS:
1. Personal Interest: Academic preparation, career advancement, personal enrichment
2. Professional Certification: Meeting external association requirements (letter required)
3. Credit Transfer: Taking courses to transfer to another institution

APPLICATION PROCESS:
- Submit during academic calendar period
- Use Special Student Application Form to Registrar's Office
- Reviewed by Department Scientific Committee
- Approved by Faculty Administrative Board
- Decision within 2 weeks

RULES AND REQUIREMENTS:
1. No degree awarded; receive transcript only
2. Credits transferable if later enrolling in degree program
3. Course registration requires instructor approval
4. Limited to ≤50% of full program per semester
5. Maximum 2 academic semesters per study cycle

ACADEMIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Undergraduate Special Student: Bachelor's degree or ongoing studies with CGPA ≥ 2.00
- Master's Special Student: Bachelor's degree with CGPA ≥ 2.00
- PhD Special Student: Master's degree with CGPA ≥ 2.70

ADDITIONAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Pass English proficiency exam or submit certificate
- Not expelled from any university for disciplinary reasons
- Cannot re-register if failed 2 courses as special student
- Subject to same regulations as regular students

CREDIT TRANSFER RULES:
1. Special Students at EPOKA:
   - Max 2 semesters for credit transfer to other institutions
   - Credits recognized if enrolling in same EPOKA program later

2. EPOKA Students at Other Institutions:
   - Max 4 courses for Bachelor students (2 per semester)
   - Max 2 courses for Master's students
   - Must obtain prior permission from department
   - Credits recognized as "T" grade
   - Forms available at Registrar's Office

INTERNSHIPS POLICY
===================

DIRECTIVE "ON INTERNSHIPS DURING UNDERGRADUATE STUDIES" (EU-DIR-016-EN)
Last Updated: 08.04.2013

Purpose: Specifies procedures for student internships at home and abroad.

DEFINITIONS:
- Intern: Student taking internship program
- Internship Advisor: Academic staff appointed by department head
- Internship Commission: 3 members (including instructor) appointed by Department Committee
- Workplace: Internship location in home country or abroad

GENERAL PRINCIPLES:
1. Internship Commission established per department (2-year term)
2. Compulsory Internship: 30-90 workdays before 3rd year (Architecture & Engineering)
3. Voluntary Internship: Begin by end of 2nd term
4. Duration: 30-90 days determined by Department Committee
5. Timing: Cannot coincide with course/exam terms
6. Expenses: Borne by student

INTERNSHIP PLACEMENT:
- Student finds own placement
- Requires Department Head approval
- Must notify advisor of workplace details
- Obtain advisor approval before starting
- Take Internship Evaluation document to workplace

ATTENDANCE AND OBLIGATIONS:
- Attendance compulsory (≤10% absence allowed)
- Follow workplace rules and schedules
- Comply with EPOKA Student Discipline Regulation
- Perform assigned tasks
- Payment terms determined by student-institution agreement
- For abroad internships: Stay in university-selected places

APPLICATION PROCESS:
1. Prior to Internship:
   - Annual information from Dean and website
   - Fill Internship Application Form (2 copies)
   - Submit required documents:
     * Application/Acceptance Form
     * Student ID photocopy

2. During Internship:
   - Prepare internship report (30-60 pages)
   - Language: Albanian or English based on department
   - Include all relevant topics and questions
   - Note unanswered questions with reasons
   - Focus on quality over quantity
   - Gain practical experience

3. After Internship:
   - Supervisor completes confidential Evaluation Document
   - Document mailed to advisor or given to student in sealed envelope
   - Student responsible for delivery to university advisor

EVALUATION:
1. Report evaluated by advisor and Internship Commission
2. Student presents to Commission (≤15 minutes)
3. Results: Successful/Failed or graded (department decision)
4. Failure requires internship repetition
5. Annual Internship Calendar prepared by departments

CREDIT CALCULATION:
- Compulsory Internship: Credits per department curriculum
- Voluntary Internships: Not credited
- Future: Online Internship Management System planned

PURCHASES POLICY
=================

DIRECTIVE "ON PURCHASES" (EU-DIR-010-EN)
Last Updated: 30.03.2021

Purpose: Defines procedures for purchasing goods, services, rentals, and construction.

SCOPE: All academic and administrative units.

PROCUREMENT PRINCIPLES:
1. All purchases within approved budget
2. Use standard Material Request Form
3. Approval hierarchy:
   - Administrative Units: Applicant → Head of Unit → Administrator
   - Faculty: Applicant → Faculty Administrator → Administrator
   - Department/Research Center: Applicant → Head → Administrator

PURCHASE METHODS:
1. Small Purchases (≤ 100,000 ALL):
   - Handled by Procurement & Logistics Office

2. Medium Purchases (> 100,000 ALL):
   - Handled by Purchase Commission (established by Rectorate)
   - 3-5 members appointed by Rectorate

3. Large Purchases/Investments:
   - Approved by Administrative Board

PROCEDURES:
1. Check inventory first (no duplication)
2. Emergency expenses: Administrator approval
3. Extraordinary expenses: Administrative Board approval
4. Routine purchases (fuel, communication, etc.): Administrator authority
5. Use approved Supplier List (updated annually)
6. Market research required for ordinary purchases

PURCHASE COMMISSION PROCEDURES:
- Sealed envelope offers
- Open cutback procedure
- Bargaining procedure

DOCUMENTATION:
1. Technical Terms:
   - Prepared by requesting unit
   - Attached to Material Request Form
   - May include drawings, samples, references

2. Administrative Terms (with Procurement Office):
   - Nature/type/amount of goods/services
   - Implementation procedure
   - Delivery date/place/form
   - Payment conditions
   - Taxes/fees responsibility
   - Estimated cost/collateral
   - Penalties for delays/violations
   - Provider requirements
   - Necessary documents

NOTIFICATION AND SIGNING:
- Winner notified at company legal address
- Provider deposits guarantees if needed
- Agreement signed with Procurement Office

EXCLUSIONS FROM TENDERS:
1. Entities banned from public institution tenders
2. Administrative Board members and staff
3. Rector, Deans, Administrator, Commission members, Procurement staff
4. Individuals within 2 years of employment termination
5. Any entity with conflict of interest

UNIVERSITY FREEDOM:
- Not bound by Public Procurement legislation
- Free to determine tender application
- Explicitly stated in all specifications

ENFORCEMENT:
- Scholarships & Special Students: By Administrator
- Internships: By Faculty Deans
- Purchases: By Administrator
- All policies: Based on University Statute
- Criteria determined by Administrative Board
