## 1. Policy on Continuous Improvement of Study Programs\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56516  Document Information\
- **Code:** EU-PLC-002-EN\
- **Effective Date:** 27.03.2018\
- **Pages:** 4\
\
### \uc0\u55356 \u57263  Purpose and Application\
- **Purpose:** Fundamental to University's mission for quality improvement\
- **Application:** All University study programs\
- **Method:** Uses qualitative and quantitative data including student/stakeholder feedback\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56523  Policy Statement\
EPOKA University systematically evaluates and improves study program quality to achieve high efficiency, effectiveness, and stakeholder satisfaction.\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56421  Responsibilities\
- Administered by: Department, Faculty, Curriculum Development Commission, Permanent Commission on Quality Assurance, Academic Senate\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56523  Definitions\
| Term | Definition |\
|------|------------|\
| Continuous Improvement | Ongoing effort to improve products, services, or processes |\
| Accreditation | Recognition status for study program meeting minimum quality standards |\
| Decanate | Executive collegial decision-making body at Faculty level |\
| Program Learning Outcome | Statements of what learner knows, understands, and can do after learning |\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56522  Policy Details\
#### 4.1 Program Accreditation\
- **Timing:** Apply 1 year before accreditation expiration\
- **Requirements:**\
  - Internal evaluation by Department\
  - External evaluation by ASCAL-appointed team\
  - All programs must be accredited for Albanian recognition\
- **Action Plan:** Departments develop improvement plans based on Accreditation Board recommendations\
- **Oversight:** Faculty oversees implementation, provides progress report within 1 year\
\
#### 4.2 Program Performance\
- **Frequency:** Annual review\
- **Data Sources:** Department assessment database including:\
  - Teaching performance indicators\
  - Research performance indicators\
  - Societal responsiveness\
  - Department annual reports\
  - Strategic measures\
- **Outcome:** Identify amendments for program success\
\
#### 4.3 Program Review\
- **Focus:** Meet national/international labor market needs\
- **Improvement Drivers:**\
  1. Institution's goals and mission\
  2. National Accreditation standards\
  3. European Standards and Guidelines (ESG)\
  4. National Qualifications Framework\
  5. Technology/skills/competency needs\
  6. Labor market and student needs\
  7. Current student feedback\
  8. Alumni feedback\
\
**Change Proposal Process:**\
1. **Department:** Proposes change \uc0\u8594  Decanate\
2. **Decanate:** Recommends change \uc0\u8594  Curriculum Development Commission\
3. **Curriculum Development Commission:** Provides feedback/approval \uc0\u8594  Permanent Commission on Quality Assurance\
4. **Permanent Commission on Quality Assurance:** Reviews \uc0\u8594  Academic Senate for final approval\
\
#### 4.4 Teaching and Courses Evaluation\
- **Frequency:** End of each semester\
- **Tools:**\
  - Instructor/Course Evaluation Survey\
  - Teaching Evaluation Form (class visits by Dean/Heads of Departments)\
- **Process:**\
  1. Results available in Epoka Interactive System\
  2. Low-rated lecturers meet with Dean to discuss issues\
  3. Decanate reviews results semesterly\
  4. Transform findings into improvement recommendations\
  5. Provide feedback to students on actions taken\
\
#### 4.5 Assessment of Program Learning Outcomes\
- **Timing:** After each academic year\
- **Method:** Review student results on assessments measuring learning outcomes\
\
#### 4.6 Assessment of Learning Outcome Validity\
- **Frequency:** Annual\
- **Administrator:** Career Planning and Alumni Office\
- **Tool:** Alumni Survey on knowledge/skills/competency applicability\
- **Process:** Summary report to Departments \uc0\u8594  Review results \u8594  Modify outcomes/objectives if needed\
\

## 2. Policy on Participation of Academic Staff in External Activities\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56516  Document Information\
- **Code:** EU-PLC-003-EN\
- **Effective Date:** 19.11.2019\
- **Latest Amendment:** 19.09.2023\
- **Pages:** 3\
\
### \uc0\u55356 \u57263  Purpose\
Recognizes importance of supporting staff in scholarly presentations while ensuring consistency with University mission and regulations.\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56523  Scope\
Establishes principles governing travel planning during academic year and summer months.\
\
### \uc0\u55356 \u57314  Implementation Field\
All academic and assisting academic staff members.\
\
### \uc0\u9992 \u65039  Travel Regulations During Teaching Period\
#### General Limits\
1. **Maximum Days:** 10 working days per teaching semester for:\
   - Academic/professional conferences\
   - Symposia, workshops, seminars\
   - Scientific activities\
   - Board service\
   - Other travel activities\
   - **Restriction:** No travel during Mid-term Exam week for Bachelor/Integrated Second Cycle programs\
\
2. **Frequency Limit:** One academic/professional event per teaching period\
   - Additional events may be approved for summer periods\
   - Events cannot fall in two consecutive weeks during semester\
\
3. **Event Duration:**\
   - **International:** \uc0\u8804 5 working days + reasonable travel time\
   - **National:** \uc0\u8804 3 working days + reasonable travel time\
   - **Extension:** Negotiate with Head of Department and Dean\
\
4. **Institutional Importance Exceptions:**\
   - Rectorate may approve participation in important events advancing:\
     - Institution's profile/reputation\
     - Department/Faculty profile/reputation\
     - Required for international collaboration\
   - Case-by-case basis\
\
5. **Teaching Mobility:**\
   - One experience per academic year during teaching period\
   - Duration: Normally \uc0\u8804 5 working days + travel days\
   - Additional summer experiences negotiable\
\
6. **Externally Funded Projects:**\
   - Outside policy scope\
   - Case-by-case basis, negotiate with Head of Department and Dean\
\
#### Planning and Reporting Requirements\
9. **Advance Planning:** Request permission and funding 30 days before travel\
10. **Post-Travel Duties:**\
    - Fulfill reporting duties\
    - Conduct Make-up Classes per Rectorate procedure\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56526  Related Documents\
1. Procedure: "Reporting and other duties of staff members who participate in conferences, seminars, symposiums, project activities, mobility programs and other related activities outside the University"\
2. Application Form on Participation in Activities held outside the University\
3. Briefing Report on Participation in Activities representing the University\
4. Narrative Report on Participation in Activities held outside the University\

## 3. Regulation on Discipline of Managing, Academic and Administrative Staff\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56516  Document Information\
- **Code:** EU-REG-016-EN\
- **Effective Date:** 10.10.2009\
- **Pages:** 11\
\
### \uc0\u55356 \u57263  Scope and Field\
- **Scope:** Defines disciplinary sanctions for leading, academic, and administrative staff\
- **Field:** Defines violation types, sanctions, competent authorities, disciplinary councils, firing, and complaint procedures\
\
### \uc0\u9878 \u65039  Disciplinary Sanctions\
| Sanction | Definition |\
|----------|------------|\
| Warning | Document indicating need for more careful work/behavior |\
| Critics | Document showing deficiencies in work/behavior |\
| Cutting monthly wage | Cut of 1/8 to 1/30 of monthly gross wage |\
| Removal from leading position | Removal from Rector, Dean, Director, Head positions |\
| Withdrawn from work | Interrupting work relations at employee's request |\
| Firing | Termination without right to return |\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u57000  Violations and Corresponding Sanctions\
#### Warning (Article 5)\
Applies for:\
- Incomplete/untimely order/duty fulfillment\
- Procedure non-compliance\
- Poor document/equipment management\
- Tardiness/early leaving without reason/permission\
- Institution measurement non-respect\
- Inappropriate behavior for position\
- Improper application/complaint procedures\
- Undignified behavior with students\
- Unauthorized publication of organ discussions/decisions\
- Untimely/unreasoned replies to competent organs\
- Non-work-related activities during work time\
- Neglect of staff professional development\
\
#### Critics (Article 6)\
Applies for:\
- Complete/timely order/duty non-fulfillment\
- Irregular document/equipment storage/usage\
- Disrespect toward leaders during duty\
- Reputation-harming attitudes/actions\
- Personal use of University tools/equipment\
- Loss of official University documents/items\
- Bickering with colleagues/support staff/students\
- Misconduct in work environments\
- Harassment of colleagues/directors\
- Unreasonable appeals to decisions/orders\
- Work regularity/peace/order disturbance\
- Non-information/disinformation on important issues\
- Private paid lessons for University students\
- Unauthorized publication of organ discussions\
\
#### Cutting Monthly Salary (Article 7)\
Applies for:\
- Complete/timely order/duty non-fulfillment\
- Irregular document/equipment management\
- Personal use of University property\
- Lies/disinformation to competent individuals\
- Offensive/humiliating actions toward staff/students\
- Reputation-harming attitudes/actions\
- Prohibited publications in work environment\
- Unauthorized leaking of University discussions/decisions\
- Benefit-seeking from supervised enterprises\
- Theft/damage of University announcements/documents\
- Forgery/use of forged documents\
- Unauthorized use of University spaces\
- Unjustified non-participation in meetings/work\
- Drunkenness/alcohol use at work\
- Improper report/document preparation\
- Unauthorized income-generating activities\
- Discrimination (language, religion, race, gender, politics, sects)\
- Purposeful non-compliance with orders/duties\
- Gift solicitation/acceptance for favors\
- Obstruction of University programs\
- Unauthorized announcement/document preparation/distribution\
- Boycott/participation in order-undermining activities\
\
#### Removal from Leading Position (Article 8)\
Applies for:\
- Pursuit of personal interests by leader\
- Non-respect/non-implementation of superior/Board decisions\
- Failure to ensure productive/peaceful work environment\
- Non-submission of annual work reports without justification\
- Non-conduction of required council meetings after warning\
- Neglect of director duties\
\
#### Withdrawn from Work (Article 9)\
Applies for:\
- Non-start of work within 15 days of appointment without permission/reason\
- 10-day absence without permission/reason (2+ days for part-time)\
- Non-attendance at 2 consecutive or 3 yearly council meetings without permission/reason\
\
#### Firing (Article 10)\
Applies for:\
- Ideological/political work order deterioration\
- Organized boycott/occupation/work prevention\
- Prohibited political/ideological material distribution\
- 20-day unauthorized absence in 1 year (3 days for part-time)\
- Violation of University personnel/students\
- Inappropriate/disgraceful conduct by academic staff\
- Membership/support of illegal organizations\
- Obstruction of learning process/examinations\
- Narcotic substance use\
- Threats/false testimony/guilt assumption\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56523  Additional Disciplinary Provisions\
#### Article 11: Unpredicted Violations\
- Competent authority may grant same measures for similar actions based on circumstances/significance\
\
#### Article 12: Violation Repetition\
- Repeated violations: One degree heavier sanction than previous\
\
#### Article 13: Positive Rating\
- Staff with "good" or "very good" rating: One degree easier sanction than normal\
\
### \uc0\u55356 \u57307  Disciplinary Councils\
#### Authority Structure (Article 14)\
- **Warning/Critics:** Person in charge of disciplinary proceedings\
- **Withdrawn/Removal:** Direct superior leader\
- **Salary Cut/Firing:** Requires consent of disciplinary proceedings person + Disciplinary Council decision + Rector order\
\
#### Council Composition (Article 15)\
- **Hierarchy Restrictions:**\
  - Assoc/Assist Professors cannot judge Professors\
  - Assist Professors cannot judge Assoc Professors\
  - Members cannot judge issues related to themselves\
- **Multiple Offenders:** Determined by highest degree/position\
\
#### Council Procedures\
- **Meetings (Article 16):** Called by President who sets place/time\
- **Quorum (Article 17):** >50% members for validity\
- **Reporting (Article 18):** Member appointed by president, prepares file within 3 days\
- **Review (Article 19):** Report \uc0\u8594  Discussion \u8594  Questioning (if needed) \u8594  Voting\
- **Voting (Article 20):** \
  - For/against only (no abstention)\
  - Absolute majority required\
  - President votes last, breaks ties\
  - Details signed by all present members\
- **Decision Authority (Article 21):** Council free to accept/reject investigation recommendations\
- **Decision Timing (Article 22):**\
  - Warning/Critics: Within 7 days of process completion\
  - Salary Cut/Firing: Within 1 month of file opening\
- **Investigation Rights (Article 23):** Council can inquire files, hear witnesses, examine evidence\
- **Decision Writing (Article 24):** Written within 5 days, signed by chairman/members\
- **Decision Communication (Article 25):** To interested person within 5 days of signing\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56613  Firing Procedures\
#### Definition (Article 26)\
- Long-term precautionary measure for staff presenting work performance risk\
- Can be taken during disciplinary procedure\
\
#### Competence (Article 27)\
- By direct supervisor who assigned duty\
\
#### Responsibility (Article 28)\
- Proceedings must begin within 10 days of reason verification\
- Legal/material responsibility for non-compliance\
\
#### Rights During Proceedings (Article 29)\
- Paid 2/3 monthly salary\
- Continue social assistance benefits\
- If fired as safeguard: Paid 1/3 salary until proceedings end\
\
#### Removal of Sanctions (Article 30)\
- Except dismissal: Sanctions hidden after proceedings\
- Dismissal: Apply Article 28 provisions\
\
#### Re-beginning Work (Article 31)\
- Required when:\
  - Dismissal precaution taken but other sanction given\
  - Investigation removed due to amnesty\
  - Precaution lasts three months\
\
#### Freedom (Article 32)\
- Dismissal can be deleted if actions not obstacle to service continuity\
\
### \uc0\u9878 \u65039  Disciplinary Procedure\
#### Responsible Persons (Article 33)\
- **Appointed Person:** Conducts proceedings directly/through others\
- **Initiation:** Rector (University-wide) or Dean (unit employees)\
- **Investigator Qualification:** Equal/higher academic title than investigated\
- **Multiple Proceedings:** Joined and judged by highest body\
\
#### Timing (Article 34)\
- **Initiation:** Within 1 month of violation knowledge\
- **Prescription:** 6 months from violation commission\
- **Management/Finance Violations:** \uc0\u8804 1 year from commission to proceedings\
\
#### Duration (Article 35)\
- **Notification:** Without delay to subject\
- **Completion:** Within 1 month from assignment notification\
- **Extension:** Request additional time, inform higher position\
\
#### Development Manner (Article 36)\
- **Methods:** Hear witnesses, hold investigations, require experts, appoint commissions\
- **Recording:** All methods recorded in registry with details\
- **Staff Obligation:** Provide required information without delay\
- **Delay Consequences:** Subject to separate disciplinary proceedings\
\
#### Right of Defense (Article 37)\
- **Requirement:** Must be given before disciplinary measures\
- **Timeframe:** 3 days given by designated person/council\
- **Non-Exercise:** Considered abandonment of right\
\
#### Report (Article 38)\
- **Contents:** Proceedings adoption, dates, identities, alleged violation, investigation stages, evidence, defense\
- **Investigation:** Each violation separately\
- **Submission:** To competent authority without delay\
\
### \uc0\u9878 \u65039  Enforcement and Complaint\
#### Enforcement (Article 39)\
- **Effect:** Immediate after granting\
- **Salary Cut:** Applied following month\
- **Announcement:** To direct superior of subject\
\
#### Complaint (Article 40)\
- **Warning/Critics:** To highest hierarchical level or disciplinary councils\
- **Decision:** Final\
\
#### Timing and Procedures (Article 41)\
- **Deadline:** 7 days from decision\
- **Non-filing:** Decision considered final\
- **Appeal Body:** Can give same, alleviate, or abolish measure\
- **Decision Timing:** Within 7 days of submission\
- **Breach/Deletion:** Must be carried out\
\
#### Deletion After Period (Article 42)\
- **Recording:** In registry\
- **Deletion:** After 2 years upon request of granting body\
- **Good Behavior:** Deleted from registry, decision attached to file\
\
### \uc0\u55357 \u56523  Additional Provisions\
#### Registration Maintenance (Article 43)\
- Notebook with marked pages for all disciplinary proceedings stages\
- Responsibility: Secretary-General or Secretaries\
\
#### File Submission (Article 44)\
- To competent person/body upon signature in confidentiality\
\
